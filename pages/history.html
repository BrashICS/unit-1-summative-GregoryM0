Steve Jobs Biography

Steve Jobs was an American inventor, designer, and entrepreneur who co-founded Apple Computer Inc. in 1976. He left the company in 1985, then founded NeXt which was a computer company which were great models for future adaptations, and a majority shareholder and owner in Pixar Animations before it was sold to Walt Disney. Steve Jobs later returned to apple and Jobs played a pivotal role in the development of revolutionary technologies such as the iPhone and iPad. He was born in 1955 in San Francisco, California, and was adopted as an infant. Jobs' early life was marked by curiosity and frustrations with formal schooling. He briefly attended Reed College before dropping out. Later Steve JObs traveled through India, seeking enlightenment before later studying Zen Buddhism. Steve Jobs also found his love of typography from creative classes which he took after dropping out of Reed College. With these new skills he took a position as a video game designer with Atari. Later, Jobs and Steve Wozniak started Apple Computer Inc. in the Jobs' family garage, and the company revolutionized the computer industry. Jobs died in 2011 following a battle with pancreatic cancer.
