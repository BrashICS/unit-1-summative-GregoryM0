Steve Jobs importance to Pixar

Steve Jobs acquired the computer graphics division of Lucasfilm, Ltd. and renamed it Pixar Animation Studios in 1986. He remained CEO and majority shareholder until it was acquired by The Walt Disney Company in 2006. After the acquisition, Jobs became Disney's largest individual shareholder and a member of Disney's Board of Directors. Additionally, he was credited as an Executive Producer in Toy Story and is often acknowledged under "Special Thanks" in the credits of Pixar's films, despite having little involvement in productions. Jobs also contributed over $5 million USD in the making of Toy Story.

Pixar's importance to Computer Science

Pixar is a very important company in the realm of computer science, and the majority of this importance is placed on their invention of CGI. CGI or Computer Generated Imagery is used to produce images for many purposes, including visual arts, advertising, for video games and films, and much more. CGI created a whole new industry through animation and has allowed us to do unbelievable feats.
